# DTR-NQ-WP-20260722-07 — Selection robustness and explicit execution contract

## Status

Claimed.

## Objective

Determine whether the corrected NQ reversal result survives a reproducible multiple-testing-aware review of the historical 904-configuration research, while replacing import-time execution monkey-patching with explicit causal public interfaces.

## Strategic questions

1. Can the original staged candidate universe be reconstructed exactly enough to align candidate returns on a common observation unit?
2. Does the selected historical candidate retain familywise evidence after accounting for candidate search?
3. How often does a resampled history select a different configuration or strategy family?
4. Are the apparent plateaus stable, or are results driven by a small number of sharp optima and concentrated date/session blocks?
5. Can every public research entry point require an explicit execution/gap policy without hidden import-order behaviour?

## Required work

### A. Candidate-universe audit

- inventory the committed 904-candidate leaderboards, configuration generators, staged base updates, and selection chronology;
- identify whether the exact original candidate paths are reproducible from current main;
- distinguish exact reconstruction from a new 904-candidate rerun;
- document missing artifacts rather than infer them silently.

### B. Aligned return matrix

- choose and preregister a common sampling unit before inspecting inference results;
- baseline unit: `session_date × session`, with zero return where a candidate takes no position;
- also report calendar-date aggregation as a sensitivity;
- use causal gap liquidation for all new reruns;
- preserve strategy costs and one-position-at-a-time sequencing;
- store candidate identity, pack, parameters, sample coverage, and return stream hashes.

### C. Selection inference

At minimum:

- centered max-t bootstrap or White-style reality check against zero;
- probability of backtest overfitting or a documented approximation when CSCV assumptions are not met;
- bootstrap reselection frequency by candidate and parameter family;
- selected-versus-neighbourhood plateau diagnostics;
- nested or rolling reselection only under a frozen candidate universe;
- fixed random seeds and deterministic repeat verification.

### D. Execution architecture

- remove package-initializer monkey-patching of engine functions;
- expose explicit causal public entry points;
- require or strongly default the execution/gap policy at the manifest and backtest boundary;
- prevent direct production-like use of historical `observe_only` and retrospective `reject_unsafe` policies;
- preserve lower-level fixture access without presenting unsafe paths as canonical;
- add import-order and direct-entry regression tests.

## Scope locks

- no strategy parameter tuning;
- no new candidate family beyond the original staged universe;
- no IFVG/CISD/continuation/entry-routing combinations;
- no use of fresh 2026 performance data;
- no deployment or Pine implementation;
- no claim that a descriptive bootstrap resolves selection pressure.

## Decision states

- `HISTORICAL_SELECTION_EVIDENCE_SURVIVES`;
- `HISTORICAL_SELECTION_EVIDENCE_WEAK_OR_AMBIGUOUS`;
- `HISTORICAL_SELECTION_EVIDENCE_FAILS`;
- `EXACT_RECONSTRUCTION_BLOCKED`.

All states retain `DO_NOT_DEPLOY` until the fresh-data and Pine-parity gates are complete.

## Acceptance gates

- candidate chronology and universe documented;
- aligned matrix construction tested and deterministic;
- inference code committed with fixed seeds;
- no hidden execution-policy monkey-patching remains in canonical imports;
- causal 495-trade benchmark remains exact;
- all prior module regression tests pass;
- pinned Ruff and Python 3.11/3.12 CI pass;
- independent adversarial review and handover complete.
