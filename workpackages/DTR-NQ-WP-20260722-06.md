# DTR-NQ-WP-20260722-06 — Baseline validity reset

## Status

Claimed.

## Objective

Correct the noncausal open-trade gap handling in the frozen NQ reversal benchmark, refreeze the strategy without parameter changes, and establish whether timestamp, rollover, selection-pressure, and concentration risks alter the research conclusions.

## Trigger

The 2026-07-22 independent review identified that `integrity.run_backtest(..., gap_policy="reject_unsafe")` simulates a trade through its future exit and then discards it when a missing-data gap occurred during the trade. The rule is not knowable at entry and can alter subsequent portfolio eligibility using future information.

## Required work

1. Replace retrospective trade rejection with a causal open-trade gap policy.
2. Add long/short fixtures for gaps before entry, during an open trade, through a stop, through scheduled close, and beyond the stop.
3. Preserve ex-ante session-range rejection and causal signal-path truncation.
4. Refreeze the NQ reversal baseline and attribute every trade change.
5. Test timestamp bar-open versus bar-close hypotheses using vendor VWAP and session alignment evidence.
6. Identify quarterly rollover dates or discontinuity candidates and run roll-adjacent exclusion stress.
7. Implement reproducible baseline uncertainty and selection-pressure diagnostics with fixed seeds.
8. Publish corrected aggregate, chronological, session, weekday, session×weekday, cost, funnel, and drawdown evidence.
9. Re-run continuation, IFVG, CISD, and entry-routing manifests without retuning after the corrected benchmark is accepted.

## Causal gap contract

- No trade may be accepted or rejected using a gap that has not yet become observable.
- A gap before entry may invalidate a session or signal only when known before the entry decision.
- When an unsafe gap becomes observable while a position is open, the engine must liquidate causally at the first observable post-gap price under a conservative gap-fill rule.
- For a long position, the liquidation price is no better than the worse of the active stop execution price and first post-gap open execution price; short logic is symmetric.
- The resulting exit time controls subsequent portfolio eligibility.
- Gap events, liquidation prices, and reason codes must be explicit in trade artifacts.
