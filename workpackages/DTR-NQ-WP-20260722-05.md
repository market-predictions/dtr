Determine whether a causally defined first-pullback entry or a predeclared hybrid router improves the frozen NQ gap-safe reversal strategy relative to its current break-close entry, without changing signal generation, stop construction, targets, time exits, or integrity policy.

## Strategic question

After the frozen reversal signal has already become valid, does entering immediately at the break close remain the best execution decision, or does waiting for the first causal pullback improve price, risk, and expectancy enough to compensate for missed trades, delayed entries, and changed position sequencing?

## Scope

1. Preserve the frozen sweep, reclaim, protected-pivot, BOS/MSS, impulse, acceptance, session, weekday, and trend-filter logic.
2. Preserve stop, TP1, runner, breakeven, scheduled close, maximum hold, costs, and gap policy.
3. Implement a causal first-pullback entry state after the existing break-close signal.
4. Define explicit pullback band, hold/rejection, expiry, excessive-extension, reset, and no-fill outcomes.
5. Test break-close and first-pullback routes as separate populations.
6. Implement one predeclared hybrid router using only information available at the break-close decision.
7. Separate signal-level opportunity analysis from implementable portfolio results.
8. Attribute missed, delayed, replaced, retained, and newly enabled trades.
9. Report entry-price improvement, stop distance, realized R, adverse/favourable excursion, latency, no-fill rate, and coverage.
10. Run chronological, cost, band-neighbourhood, expiry-neighbourhood, and walk-forward checks.
11. End with an independent `PROMOTE_ENTRY_ROUTE`, `HOLD_FOR_FRESH_DATA`, or rejection decision.

## Predeclared structural routes

- `ENTRY_BREAK_CLOSE`: frozen baseline route.
- `ENTRY_FIRST_PULLBACK`: first valid revisit of a narrow structural band after the frozen signal.
- `ENTRY_HYBRID_PREDECLARED`: use break close only when the signal bar closes near the broken structure; otherwise wait for the first pullback.

Neighbourhood values may be evaluated for robustness, but the hybrid decision contract must be declared before full-result inspection.

## Non-goals

- No changes to reversal signal qualification.
- No changes to stops or exits.
- No CISD or IFVG rescue combination.
- No continuation combination.
- No session, weekday, direction, or regime optimization.
- No production or Pine implementation.
- No new data provider.

## Acceptance criteria

- Frozen break-close baseline reproduces exactly.
- Pullback state uses no future bars or retrospective fills.
- Every no-fill, expiry, reset, invalidation, and excessive-extension outcome is counted.
- Portfolio sequencing is simulated independently for every route.
- Entry-route comparisons include opportunity coverage and changed-trade attribution.
- Parameter neighbourhoods precede any promotion.
- Cost and chronological evidence precede any promotion.
- Pinned Ruff and pytest pass on Python 3.11 and 3.12.
- Independent review records a final decision.

## Promotion rule

A route may be promoted only if it improves the development, validation, and later-research decision profile with acceptable opportunity coverage, stable band/expiry neighbourhoods, realistic costs, and no unexplained sequencing effects. Aggregate net R alone is insufficient.
