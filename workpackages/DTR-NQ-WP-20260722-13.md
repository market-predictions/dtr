# DTR-NQ-WP-20260722-13 — E6 event, holiday and rollover attribution

## Objective

Measure whether the frozen E6 trade stream behaves materially differently around official FOMC, CPI, Employment Situation/NFP, quarterly expiration, customary roll, early-close and shortened-holiday periods.

## Decision boundary

This is attribution-only historical research. It cannot create an event exclusion, alter E6, authorize Pine, change sizing or support deployment.

## Frozen inputs

- E6 signal and execution logic unchanged.
- Tuesday–Friday; Asia, London and New York.
- One global open position at a time.
- Normal cost: one tick slippage each side and $2.25 commission each side.
- Registered NQ archive SHA-256 must reproduce exactly.
- Official date sources are Federal Reserve, BLS and CME Group.

## Required outputs

- exact E6 regression;
- official event calendar and provenance;
- event-day and pre/post-release attribution;
- expiration-week, roll-window, early-close and holiday-shortened attribution;
- detected discontinuity evidence around customary roll dates;
- year/session/direction breakdowns;
- paired date-block uncertainty;
- independent reconstruction and deterministic repeat;
- report, review, status, roadmap, changelog, decision ledger and handover.

## Stop rules

- Any baseline or checksum mismatch stops execution.
- Samples below 10 trades are anecdotal; below 30 are low-sample.
- No category may be converted into a filter on 2023–2025 data.
- No neighboring event window or alternate date definition may be searched after results.
