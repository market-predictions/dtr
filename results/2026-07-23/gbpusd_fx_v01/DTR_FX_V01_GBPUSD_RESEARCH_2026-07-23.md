# DTR-FX v0.1 — GBPUSD bounded architecture test

Date: 2026-07-23  
Data: Dukascopy GBPUSD M1 BID/ASK, 2022-01-01 through 2025-12-31  
Decision: `NO_ARM_PASSES_DTR_FX_V01_PROMOTION`

## Purpose

Test whether the DTR sweep–reclaim–pivot–BOS concept becomes viable on GBPUSD after replacing the index-futures session map with London-local FX liquidity references and using side-correct bid/ask execution.

## Frozen evidence partitions

- Development: 2022–2023
- Confirmation: 2024
- Locked evaluation: 2025

No arm, threshold, direction or session was selected from the 2025 result.

## Data and execution contract

- 2,103,840 synchronized full-grid minutes.
- 1,488,077 active bid/ask minutes.
- No duplicate timestamps.
- Corrected documented Dukascopy BI5 field-order defect.
- Median active spread: 0.9 pip.
- Development 80th-percentile spread gates: 1.2 pips London, 1.1 pips New York.
- London-local session boundaries, including UK daylight-saving changes.
- Five-minute midpoint signal bars.
- Next active one-minute side-correct entry: ask for longs, bid for shorts.
- 0.1 pip adverse slippage per side.
- $3.50 commission per side per standard lot.
- Minimum four-pip stop and total estimated execution cost no greater than 15% of initial risk.
- Conservative stop-first ordering when stop and target occur in one minute.
- One open position per arm.

## Promotion decision

No arm passed the complete gate. Every arm failed the 150-trade minimum and every date-block 95% interval crossed zero. The three positive full-period arms also failed stability:

- The Asian pullback arm was positive in development and 2024 but lost 2.99R in 2025.
- The previous-day London arm was the strongest coherent mechanism, but 2025 was slightly negative and its interval remained wide.
- The Asian 1.5R arm was concentrated in 2022 and negative in both 2024 and 2025.

The New York arms did not provide a viable baseline. The London-range/New-York arm improved in 2024–2025 only after being strongly negative in 2022–2023, indicating regime instability rather than a stable edge.

## Mechanism diagnostics

The previous-day London arm was positive on both directions:

- Long: 30 trades, +2.76R, +0.092R expectancy.
- Short: 25 trades, +6.93R, +0.277R expectancy.

The other positive London arms were driven mainly by shorts. This is diagnostic only. A short-only strategy was not preregistered and is not promoted.

The evidence supports three conclusions:

1. London-local liquidity mapping materially improves the direct-transfer result.
2. Previous-day extreme sweeps are more coherent than the tested Asian-midpoint BOS exit.
3. The available sample is too small and unstable to establish a tradable GBPUSD edge.

## Verification

- A second complete run generated byte-identical trade ledgers, summaries, funnel counts, audit and decision files.
- An independent code path reconstructed every trade P&L, stop/target geometry, non-overlap condition and aggregate metric.
- Maximum P&L reconstruction difference was below 8e-13R.
- Independent verdict: `PASS`.

This is an independent programmatic review, not an external human, broker or legal audit.

## Research decision and next gate

Do not tune the six arms on 2022–2025 and do not promote a short-only rule from the attribution.

Freeze `FX_B1_PREVDAY_LONDON_BOS_MID` as the sole fixed challenger for a new evidence package. Test it unchanged on older GBPUSD bid/ask history, preferably 2015–2021, and preserve 2026 onward as prospective evidence. The research line should stop if the older-history expectancy is non-positive, cost-fragile, or concentrated in one subperiod.

No Pine implementation, sizing or deployment is authorized.
