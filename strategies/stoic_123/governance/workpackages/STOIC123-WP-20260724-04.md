## Strategic question

Does the apparent improvement from restricting NQ no-map long entries to regular trading hours reflect a reproducible session mechanism, and is a simple RTH EMA-break entry superior to the full Stoic 1-2-3 sequence?

## Scientific boundary

The 2022-2025 NQ RTH attribution was discovered after the original validation. The existing 2015-2021 and 2026 Dukascopy long comparator has also been inspected. Those partitions may therefore be used only for a mechanism screen.

A promotable proxy result requires source-qualified, previously uninspected Dukascopy USATECH history from 2010-2014. Performance code must not run on those partitions until deterministic checksums are committed into the frozen design.

## Frozen session contract

- Source timestamps: UTC.
- Session timezone: `America/New_York` using IANA daylight-saving rules.
- RTH entry window: `09:30:00` inclusive through `16:00:00` exclusive.
- The filter applies to entry signals only.
- Position management, stops, opposite-direction technical exits and maximum-hold exits remain active across the full session.
- No RTH subwindow or intraday-hour search is permitted.

## Comparators

- full-session full 1-2-3 long;
- overnight-entry full 1-2-3 long;

- RTH EMA break plus retest;
- fifty deterministic RTH matched-time long controls per primary and secondary candidate.

## Source qualification gate

The source-only workflow must publish, for 2010, 2011, 2012, 2013 and 2014:

- deterministic compressed-file SHA-256;
- active row count and timestamp bounds;
- zero-volume removal count;
- duplicate and OHLC integrity audit;
- explicit proxy classification.

If the product does not have sufficient historical coverage, the fresh-history promotion screen is blocked. The inspected mechanism screen may still be completed but cannot authorize paid NQ validation.

## Robustness tests

For both primary and secondary candidates:

- one-tick baseline and two-tick cost stress;
- one-minute and five-minute entry delay;
- year attribution;
- date-block bootstrap;
- return-to-drawdown and maximum drawdown;
- matched-time RTH controls;
- exposure and exit-reason attribution where supported by the frozen ledger.

## Decision boundary

Passing the proxy screen can only justify a later preregistered actual-NQ futures validation. It cannot promote a trading strategy.

The full 1-2-3 sequence must add at least `0.05R` expectancy or improve return-to-drawdown by at least 20% versus RTH EMA-break-only on fresh history, without underperforming on the 2014 holdout. Otherwise the sequence complexity is rejected.

## Restrictions

- No session-window, EMA-length, weekday, volatility, stop, target, delay, timeframe or exit search.
- No pooled evidence across fresh, holdout and inspected partitions.
- No CME NQ claim from proxy data.
- No Pine, sizing, alerts, paper deployment or live trading.
