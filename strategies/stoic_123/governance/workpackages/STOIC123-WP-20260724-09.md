## Objective

Allow multiple strategy variations to share one qualified source load, one derived-bar cache and reusable detection artifacts without exposing holdouts or allowing a parameter sweep to authorize promotion.

## Delivered

- Typed discovery-only batch-design contract.
- Strict prohibition of promotion and holdout access in batch mode.
- Validation of supported `SequenceConfig` overrides and duplicate variation IDs.
- Full-configuration and detection-dependency signatures.
- Detection grouping for variations that share signal logic but differ in execution settings.
- Shared-cache batch screening runner.
- Per-variation futility status, candidate metrics, diagnostic metrics and configuration lineage.
- Unit tests for contract loading, override application, detection grouping, holdout protection and unknown-field rejection.

## Research boundary

No real Stoic parameter sweep was executed. The mechanical Stoic family remains closed, so using the new batch engine to mine this inspected dataset would violate the research decision. The infrastructure is available for a genuinely new preregistered mechanism and a designated discovery partition.

## Performance decision

A Numba simulator was not added. Real-data profiling showed only `5.03s` in ordinary simulation during a `52.56s` full certification; matched controls, source loading and detection were materially larger. JIT work is deferred until a future profiler shows simulation as a dominant cost and a trade-by-trade reference parity suite can justify the additional maintenance burden.
