## Strategic question

Can we establish a factor-diverse, execution-ready FX source universe large enough to test the claimed previous-day external-liquidity sweep-and-reclaim mechanism without relying on one pair or one USD factor?

## Frozen universe

- USD/European majors: EURUSD, GBPUSD, USDCHF.
- USD/commodity majors: AUDUSD, NZDUSD, USDCAD.
- JPY block: USDJPY, EURJPY, GBPJPY.
- European cross: EURGBP.

All ten instruments are mandatory. Any replacement requires a documented source failure and a new preregistration before event returns are inspected.

## Source period

- 2015–2021: discovery/event census.
- 2022–2023: event-study validation.
- 2024–2025: untouched holdout.
- 2026 YTD through 2026-07-23: monitoring only.

## Source contract

- Provider: Dukascopy.
- Resolution: one minute.
- Sides: BID and ASK.
- Correct BI5 order: seconds, open, close, low, high, volume.
- Canonical output: timestamp, open, high, low, close, volume, active flag.
- Inactive records are preserved.
- Every annual side receives an immutable SHA-256 and audit.
- Raw source remains private and outside Git.

## Qualification gates

For every symbol and partition:

- canonical OHLC integrity;
- zero duplicate timestamps and strictly increasing time;
- active flag exactly matches positive volume;
- BID/ASK timestamp synchronization of at least 99.9%;
- no negative executable open or close spreads on synchronized active rows;
- adequate synchronized active coverage and month breadth;
- explicit pip-normalized spread statistics.

## Next gate

After all source hashes are frozen, run a source-independent event census for conditional previous-day high/low sweeps and prompt reclaims.

The event census must report pooled, per-pair, factor-block and date-clustered evidence. Fewer than 100 pooled events is an immediate stop for the SB-1 architecture.

## Restrictions

No event-return inspection, strategy P&L, pair selection, pair-specific tuning, holdout access, SB-1 execution, Pine work, sizing, alerts, paper deployment or live use is authorized in this work package.
