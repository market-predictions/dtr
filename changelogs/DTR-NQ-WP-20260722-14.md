# Changelog — DTR-NQ-WP-20260722-14

## v0.4.0-research.14 — 2026-07-22

### Added

- $100,000 fixed-fraction account simulation for 0.50%, 1.00% and 1.50% current-equity risk.
- Normal, two-tick-per-side and four-tick-per-side execution-cost scenarios with commissions preserved.
- Historical final-equity, maximum-drawdown, losing-streak and time-under-water evidence.
- 20,000 ETH-date-block and 20,000 month-block resamples for every risk/cost combination.
- Final-equity, drawdown, loss-probability, losing-streak and time-under-water distributions.
- Frozen warning labels for loss and 10%, 20% and 30% drawdown tail risk.
- Independent 10,000-run reconstruction under different seeds.
- Deterministic repeat, report, review, handover and permanent decision record.

### Research result

- Normal-cost historical final equity: $126,940 / $159,185 / $197,231 at 0.50% / 1.00% / 1.50% risk.
- Normal-cost historical drawdown: 4.3% / 8.4% / 12.4%.
- Severe-cost historical final equity: $117,082 / $135,430 / $154,787.
- Severe-cost historical drawdown: 5.3% / 10.3% / 15.2%.
- Normal-cost 1.00% risk had a resampled 95th-percentile maximum drawdown of approximately 17.9–20.7%.
- Severe-cost 1.00% risk had a 10.6–16.6% probability of reaching 20% drawdown.
- Severe-cost 1.50% risk reached 20% drawdown in approximately 45.1–54.9% of resamples and 30% drawdown in approximately 8.5–13.7%.

### Decision

- Record `EQUITY_COST_STRESS_COMPLETE_NO_SIZING_AUTHORIZATION`.
- Preserve 0.50% and 1.00% only as paper-research risk envelopes.
- Classify 1.50% as aggressive rather than a balanced default.
- Do not optimize further risk fractions or sizing rules on the current sample.

### Known limits

- Historical sequence and resamples derive from the same 2023–2025 research sample.
- Source timestamp and continuous-contract methodology remain unresolved.
- Fresh post-2025 NQ evidence is unavailable.
- Results do not model exchange margin changes, liquidity shocks beyond fixed slippage, or whole-contract sizing constraints.
